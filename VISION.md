# HeroByte North Star — The End-Game Vision

HeroByte becomes the virtual tabletop that plays like the games that made us fall in love with adventures: the whole group joins free in a browser in thirty seconds, the DM paints or *generates* a living world — overworld to town to tavern cellar — in one coherent SNES tile aesthetic, and every map arrives at the table already alive, with working doors, vision, light, and fog, no reconstruction, no modules, no sysadmin. It fuses Owlbear's instant-join ease with the table power people buy Foundry for, wrapped in an art direction nobody else owns, on a platform where many groups play at once and every campaign is a single versioned file the DM keeps forever. And it kills the number-one cost of being a DM — prep — because the world can be generated *mid-session*, one keystroke after the party kicks in a door you never prepped. Where classic pixels meet modern play.

## The Pitch in One Session

**Tuesday, 9 PM.** Maya has forty minutes to prep Saturday's game. She opens her table from her cartridge shelf and clicks the **Atlas** tab: her campaign is a tree — World, the Verdant Coast, Port Meridian, and the docks district where last session ended. The smugglers' warehouse the party is about to hit doesn't exist yet; its node just says so. She clicks it, and a JRPG dialog asks: *"Nothing lies within… yet. [Generate Building] [Link Existing Map] [Leave Dark]."* She picks Generate, sets *warehouse, two floors, one cellar*, and the interior appears in seconds — walls, doors, crates, lantern light — its footprint matching the building outline the players saw from the street. It's already good. She makes it hers: grabs the Terrain Brush and paints the cellar floor to wet stone, the autotiler knitting edges as she drags. She drops a **Living Stamp** called *Jail Cell* from her Shelf — it lands with a working iron door and a flickering torch. She drags four smugglers from her Bestiary into a **Backstage Pocket** named *Warehouse Ambush*, draws its trigger region by the stairs, flips the map's Mood to *Night*, and sends her players invite links. Total prep: eleven minutes. She spends the other twenty-nine on the villain's voice.

**Saturday, 7 PM.** Players click their links and pick their characters from identity cards — no accounts, no forms — and their tokens pop into the spawn zone with soft staggered thumps. The fog is real: they see the warehouse only as far as their light allows. The rogue clicks a door; it creaks open on every screen and the darkness beyond peels back. She finds the seam in the wall, and Maya presses one button: the secret door de-pixelates into existence with a discovery chime the whole table hears at once. Then the fighter blunders into the trigger region. Maya's screen — only hers — pulses: *"SPRING IT?"* One click. The ambush deploys, and every player's screen does the **Boss Wipe** — a mosaic pixel-wipe, a battle sting, the initiative strip sliding in like an ATB bar, turn order already rolled. The barbarian crits: the die slow-zooms, gold flashes, the Crit Chord hits, and five screens shake in the same breath. Maya taps *Cutlass — 1d6+3* on a smuggler's card, clicks the fighter, and his HP bar drains in chunky ticks under a floating damage number. Two inputs.

Then the table goes off-script, as tables do: *"We torch the warehouse and chase him into the sewers."* Maya has no sewers. She presses **G**. *Sewer, medium, adjacent.* **ROLL.** Seconds later the party is standing in dripping, fog-shrouded tunnels that did not exist a moment ago — walled, lit, playable — and her players will never know the difference. When the boss falls, the Victory Screen lines up their portraits over the fanfare while loot types out letter by letter. She taps **Rest**: the canvas dims around a pixel campfire and everyone goes to get water. After the session, the campaign auto-ejects a versioned cartridge, and the **Save Screen** — biggest crit, total damage, the night's cursed d20 — gets posted to the group Discord before she's finished her tea.

No other product can do that whole loop. That's the point.

## Pillar 1: The Living World

The campaign is a navigable graph of linked maps — world, region, town, building, dungeon level — every node generatable in seconds, editable like a hand-made map, and regenerable without losing your edits. The founding architectural rule: **generators emit MapDocument elements, never rasters.** The editor is the generator's output format, which is why generated content is editable, publishable, and alive by construction.

- **The Atlas [LAUNCH].** `AtlasNode { kind: world|region|settlement|building|dungeon|wilderness, mapDocumentId, parentId, seed, recipeId, discovered }` and `MapLink { anchor, linkType, visibleToPlayers }`, persisted in the room snapshot so a campaign export carries its entire world. DM-side it's a file-select tree; on live maps, links render as door, stair, and signpost sprites. Players get a *discovered-only* world map — undiscovered nodes are stripped per-recipient by the same snapshot-filtering pipeline that already hides NPCs, so a curious player reading frames learns nothing.
- **Ungenerated nodes are promises [LAUNCH].** An ungenerated node costs ~100 bytes. Town generation places forty buildings, and every one is a door the party can kick in later. The world map is playable immediately; the towns on it are promises the engine keeps on demand.
- **Recipes [LAUNCH].** Pure, seeded server-side functions in a DI-registered `domains/generation`: `(seed, params) -> MapDocument + AtlasNodes + MapLinks`. Deterministic by contract, enforced with golden-snapshot tests — recipes are the most unit-testable code in the product. Shipping order: **dungeon** (rooms, corridors, secret doors, brazier lights — born with wall/door/light data the compiled scene makes live), **building interiors** (tavern/shop/warehouse grammars; footprints inherited from town outlines so the inn inside matches the inn outside), **wilderness/forest** (canopy blobs, paths, clearings, fordable river), **town** (roads, districts, walls, buildings-as-nodes), **world** (biome bands, regions, settlement markers). Mansion and castle grammar depth [BEYOND].
- **Stocked, not just drawn [LAUNCH].** Recipes emit an encounter manifest — Bestiary template references with spawn markers on the GM Notes layer, loot notes with `visibleToPlayers: false`. Geometry-only generation is the gap every competitor has; we ship rooms *with keys and inhabitants*. Deep narrative stocking (keyed prompts, faction logic) [BEYOND].
- **Scene state that survives travel [LAUNCH].** Each published node owns a `SceneState`: token positions, door states, drawings, per-player fog memory, combat state. One active scene per room at launch; traveling through a link suspends the old state and resumes the destination's, behind an SNES iris-wipe. Going back upstairs finds the room exactly as you left it. Simultaneous split-party scenes [BEYOND].
- **Keep this, reroll that [LAUNCH / BEYOND].** Every generated element carries `provenance { recipeId, seed, pass }`; any hand-edit flags it `pinned`. LAUNCH: reroll a whole node while preserving every pinned element. BEYOND: marquee-region reroll with constraint solving that reflows geometry around your renovations — a hard problem we sequence honestly instead of promising cheaply.
- **Travel mode [BEYOND].** Caravan token on the overworld, per-biome movement costs, per-region encounter tables; an encounter hit offers one click into a freshly generated wilderness battlemap via the standard link transition.
- **Wire reality, designed in [LAUNCH].** Terrain is stored as RLE-compressed 16x16 chunks; documents are fetched over HTTP with a WS notify; generation lands as batch commands under the existing `baseRevision` protocol (one undo step). Only the active compiled scene is ever broadcast — the Atlas is metadata — so the 750KB snapshot guard and 1MB message cap are respected by design, not luck.

## Pillar 2: The Forge

The SNES tile studio: paint the idea, get the art. This replaces nothing — the upload-an-image-and-align-the-grid flow stays for DMs with their own maps — but the Forge is the native path, and it's why every HeroByte map looks like it shipped on the same cartridge.

- **The Terrain Brush [LAUNCH].** DMs never place individual tiles. They pick a terrain — *Stone Floor, Dungeon Wall, River, Grass* — and paint. A pure-function autotiler (guardrail-sized, exhaustively unit-tested) resolves each cell's neighborhood to the **47-blob** corner-and-edge standard, composed RPG-Maker-style from quarter-tile sheets. Adjacency rules are authored *once by us, per tileset* — DMs never see a rule editor. One stroke commits as one batch command: one undo step. This is LDtk's celebrated killer feature inside a live multiplayer canvas — a category first. Hex snap math ships [LAUNCH]; hex autotile masks [BEYOND].
- **Terrain paints gameplay [LAUNCH].** Each terrain declares `blocksMovement/blocksVision/difficult` defaults, so painting "Dungeon Wall" *is* authoring vision blockers. The two-sources-of-truth problem is solved at compile time: the SceneCompiler merges terrain blocking and wall elements into one canonical occlusion set, and live systems consume only compiled geometry.
- **An honest renderer [LAUNCH].** The editor canvas migrates from plain SVG to a shared canvas/WebGL tile renderer (atlas UVs, a 300ms shared animation clock for water and torchlight) used by both the editor and the live table, budgeted inside the 175KB entry guard and the role-split discipline. SVG remains the export path, so raster exports inherit every fidelity improvement.
- **The Shelf [LAUNCH].** The asset panel: Terrain / Props / Structures / Stamps / My Stuff, with search, tags, favorites. **Drag = grid-snapped** with declared cell footprints and 90-degree rotation on `R`. **Alt-drag = off-grid free placement** at 1px precision with 15-degree steps and scale via the shipped TransformGizmo — serializing to the schema's existing, currently dormant `stamp` element. A seeded **scatter brush** [LAUNCH] flings rubble, bones, and mushrooms with randomized rotation and jitter, undo-exact.
- **Living Stamps [LAUNCH].** Select a furnished corner — walls, door, torch, GM note — and *Save as Stamp*. Stamps carry the full element schema, knit their terrain into surroundings via the same bitmask math on drop, and — because publish compiles rather than flattens — a dropped Jail Cell has a door that opens and a light that flickers at the table. No prefab system anywhere survives the trip to the play surface. Ours *is* the play surface.
- **The art plan, said out loud [LAUNCH track].** The moat is a first-party tileset, and it is the longest pole in the schedule: four launch packs (*Dungeon Depths, Greenwood, Hearth & Hall, Crypts & Curses*) produced by a commissioned pixel artist and/or licensed library, every asset carrying license metadata per the roadmap's requirement, all authored against a 64-color master palette checked in as `palette.json` and lint-enforced the way LOC is. This track starts on day one of the roadmap, not at the end.
- **Palette-Swap Moods [LAUNCH].** Because packs are indexed-color, one dropdown remaps the palette per scene: Day / Dusk / Night / Corrupted / Snowfall. Same tiles, new world, zero re-authoring — the village greys and festers in one click when the lich wins. Deeply SNES-authentic, and possible precisely because publish stopped flattening.
- **Uploads and imports [LAUNCH].** Drop a PNG on the Shelf: it lands in My Stuff via the asset service (presigned upload, SHA-256 dedup, quota). We support the **RPG Maker A2 quarter-tile format** as an import spec [LAUNCH] with a license-respecting stance — curated, legally-cleared packs, never "import the internet." Arbitrary tilesheet formats [BEYOND]. Signed `.hbpack` files travel inside campaign exports by content-addressed reference. Backup JSON finally round-trips (import with `schemaVersion` migration) [LAUNCH]; UVTT export for Foundry-bound maps [BEYOND].
- **Editor chrome [LAUNCH]** — acknowledged as backlog, shipped as vision's floor: a Layers panel binding the already-implemented `updateLayer/moveLayer` APIs, the orphaned MapElementInspector reborn as a right-rail inspector, Ctrl+Z/Y/Delete, eyedropper, autosave chip, and a Player Preview toggle through the player-safe render path. Terrain painting works with touch on tablets [LAUNCH]. Every brush stroke plays a soft sampled tap through the S-DSP bus — *Mario Paint, not AutoCAD*. Co-building with presence cursors [BEYOND], built honestly on the existing optimistic-locking revision protocol.

## Pillar 3: Running the Game

The console feel: prep is where complexity lives; play is where it disappears. The product invariant, audited in design review for every feature: **any action a DM performs more than once per session takes at most two inputs.**

- **Publish compiles, never flattens [LAUNCH] — the keystone.** A pure `SceneCompiler` in `packages/shared` turns a MapDocument into a `CompiledScene` — pretty background *plus* structured walls, doors, lights, notes, and spawn zones the server enforces. This fulfills the north-star comment already written in `mapStudio.ts` and the roadmap's Phase 3 exit ("publish yields a playable scene with zero manual reconstruction"). Republish diffs against the live scene and preserves tokens, always.
- **Vision and fog, free and first-party [LAUNCH].** A server `VisionService` shadowcasts player-token sightlines against compiled occlusion (a ~200-segment budget). Costed honestly: today's snapshot builder emits two variants (DM/player); vision makes it per-recipient, and the high-frequency drag/pointer preview channels must be vision-filtered too — a scoped, contract-tested change we price into the roadmap rather than hand-wave. Fog renders with SNES ordered dithering; explored-but-unseen areas desaturate as memory fog; the DM sees everything with a scanline shimmer marking what players see. Manual Reveal/Conceal brushes cover theater-of-the-mind rooms. Roll20 paywalls this; Owlbear outsources it; we ship it built-in.
- **Doors are toys [LAUNCH].** Compiled doors are clickable sprites: creak on open, clunk-and-shake when locked, per-door "players may open," and secret doors render as wall to players until revealed.
- **The DM Deck [LAUNCH].** In-session, the tabbed DM Menu gives way to a persistent JRPG battle-menu strip — **Scene, Fog, Encounter, Secrets, Backstage, Rest** — hotkeys 1–6, each mode one primary interaction, living in the existing lazy DM chunk so players never download it. The DM Menu survives as the *prep* surface, opened between sessions, never during.
- **Backstage Pockets [LAUNCH].** Encounters are chips: named bundles of tokens, notes, and an optional trigger region, authored in prep (the Map Studio GM Notes layer is the authoring surface) or on the fly. Drag a chip onto the map and the whole encounter deploys, positions preserved, tokens landing with staggered thumps. An armed trigger never auto-springs — it offers the DM a pulsing *"SPRING IT?"* toast. Pockets live in the room snapshot and travel with the save.
- **The Battle Strip [LAUNCH].** Combat replaces the modal with a portrait strip: Space advances turns, `K` marks defeated (pixel-skull badge), chips focus the camera, optional turn timers tick their last five seconds. A rotating diamond cursor — the universal JRPG "your turn" — marks the active token on every screen. The mechanics are table stakes; the presentation is the moat, and we know which is which.
- **The damage loop [LAUNCH].** Bestiary templates (activating the `templateId` already reserved in the shared schema) carry up to six named dice moves — *Bite, 1d8+4*. Tap the move, click a target: the roll lands in the shared log with full juice and the HP applies through the existing temp-HP-first model with a floating damage number. Two inputs from intent to outcome — the exact loop Alchemy RPG's reviews say it fumbled. Per-room monster HP display policy: exact, bloodied, or hidden [LAUNCH]. An SRD-safe starter Bestiary with original pixel token art [BEYOND].
- **Secrets, whispers, and handouts [LAUNCH].** One verb for every hidden thing: the **Reveal Ritual** fires `reveal-secret` on a `RevealableRef` union (`npc | door | note | region`), and the entity de-pixelates into existence with a discovery chime — the server mostly flips `visibleToPlayers` flags it already filters on. Alongside it, the quiet tools DMs actually use every ten minutes: **private rolls** (any roll flagged public / DM-only / blind), **whispers** (send a note or image to one player), and **handouts** (show the villain's face or the letter to the table). All filtered server-side, per recipient, like everything secret.
- **Pacing [LAUNCH].** *Scene* switches between published nodes with SceneState suspend/resume and a crossfade; combat state persists across server restarts (closing the documented save/load inconsistency). *Rest* dims the canvas around a four-frame campfire, pauses timers, and gives the DM a scratchpad — the "stop optimizing, stretch your legs" signal no VTT serves. The **Save Screen** [LAUNCH] renders the session's log — biggest crit, damage totals, the cursed die — as an exportable recap card: the shareable artifact. Full journal export as markdown [BEYOND].
- **Co-DM [LAUNCH].** `role: dm | co-dm | player` lands as one deliberate shared-schema migration with a defined permission matrix (co-DMs run the table; only the owner ejects cartridges or touches room settings), enforced by the existing DMAuthorizationEnforcer and honored by the snapshot filter and code-split boundaries. The map-studio roadmap's two-concurrent-DMs KPI assumes it; we scope it as the migration project it is.

## Pillar 4: Fun & Juice

The table performs. Dice rolls are attack animations, combat has ceremonies, and the whole group feels the same moment — synchronized, accessible, and governable, because juice that excludes or exhausts people isn't juice.

- **The juice-event channel [LAUNCH].** `{ t: "juice-event", kind, sourceId, targetIds, intensity, seed }` — a validated shared message on the *transient* fast path (like pointer previews), never the debounced snapshot channel, never persisted to disk. A shared seed makes particle scatter and pitch jitter identical everywhere; playback is scheduled against the RTT the HeartbeatManager already measures "for future latency metrics" — that future is now. Honest claim: Foundry modules can synchronize effects; ours is the first *first-party, deterministic, zero-config* spectacle layer that survives every update because there's no module ecosystem to shatter.
- **The JuiceDirector [LAUNCH].** One client module owns it all: a priority queue (crits preempt footsteps), category mixers (music / foley / UI / moments) with music auto-ducking under live voice, centralized photosensitivity enforcement (nothing flashes above 3Hz, ever), and reduced-motion users receiving the *information* of every moment — color, card, sound — without the movement. Governance is a feature: a DM intensity dial, a solemn-scene switch, per-player emote mutes. Foley is sparse by default; four-hour sessions are the design target, not the failure mode.
- **Dice as ritual [LAUNCH].** Pixel dice tumble across a tray layer and land on the server-validated result (the animation is deterministic cosmetics; RollLog stays truth). Nat 20: slow-zoom, gold flash, chunky *CRITICAL!!*, and the **Crit Chord** — one multi-sample stinger reserved exclusively for natural 20s until it's Pavlovian. Nat 1: a sad thud and the die visibly cracks. **Roll macros** live on the player snapshot (`{name, formula, advantage}`): saved rolls become ability buttons, and advantage renders as two dice racing.
- **The Status Screen [LAUNCH].** Press a portrait and an FF6-style menu slides in: portrait, name in the pixel font, HP as a segmented bar that drains in ticks, the 40 status badges arranged like ailment icons, macro buttons. Honestly scoped as a *view* over existing models — plus one optional `level` field added in the same schema migration so **Level Up** can be a real ceremony (rising portrait, fanfare, gold text). Stats and inventory [BEYOND].
- **Ceremonies [LAUNCH].** The **Boss Wipe** opens combat: a synchronized mosaic wipe (implemented as drawImage downscale/upscale with smoothing off — no pixel readback, immune to CORS-tainted canvases), battle sting, Battle Strip slide-in. The **Victory Screen** closes it: portraits lined up over the fanfare, a DM-typed loot ticker printing character by character. Combat gets an opening *and* a closing; no VTT marks either.
- **Presence [LAUNCH].** An emote wheel on long-press/right-click — finally giving the orphaned ContextMenu its life — pops chunky speech bubbles: reaction without unmuting. An optional `portraitTalking` second frame upgrades the existing mic glow to a two-frame mouth-flap. Joining is a moment: staging-zone spawn with a nameplate slide and a soft party-member sting. Ping flavors [BEYOND].
- **Sound and music [LAUNCH].** All SFX remain sample-based swappable WAVs in `public/sfx` through the S-DSP echo emulation — never FM synthesis, house law. Adaptive music ships as four first-party seamless OGG loops (Explore / Tension / Battle / Victory) routed through the *same* echo bus, so music and effects share one room acoustic; combat auto-cues Battle and Victory. DM-uploaded loops [BEYOND].
- **The CRT engine [LAUNCH].** The scanline costume becomes a presentation engine: a WebGL texture pass over the composited canvas — scanlines, slot-mask triads, bloom, two-frame phosphor persistence — with Arcade / Living Room / Off presets and an FPS-probe fallback to today's CSS filter. No barrel distortion on the interactive canvas (hit-testing honesty); curvature is reserved for the spectator TV frame.
- **16-bit-ify [LAUNCH, after uploads].** One click quantizes any portrait or token to the master palette with a 1px outline — a photo of someone's dog becomes a legible SNES sprite, and aesthetic coherence survives user content.
- **Accessibility as identity [LAUNCH].** SFX captions printed in the roll log (*door creaks*), colorblind-safe ring *patterns* not just hues, full keyboard traversal, and a DOM mirror of turn order and rolls for screen readers. Android haptics on juice events ship; iOS's absence is stated, not hidden.
- **Attract Mode [BEYOND].** A read-only, token-scoped spectator URL renders the session inside a pixel CRT television with a roll ticker and idle auto-pan — one click to embed in OBS. The north star for the identity work, not a launch dependency.

## Pillar 5: The Platform

Your campaign is a cartridge, the table is always on, and the whole group plays free in thirty seconds. Rooms are genuinely switch-flipping — the registry, per-room stores, and roomId-aware auth handshake exist. Accounts, durable persistence, and payments are new construction, and we say so.

- **Rooms [LAUNCH].** *NEW TABLE* mints a room (nanoid, creator is DM, per-room scrypt secrets stored with metadata); AuthenticationHandler drops its "Unknown room" rejection and routes by roomId behind the existing `whenReady()` hydration guard; `ROOM_STORE=redis` becomes the production default; idle rooms unload after 30 minutes. **Cross-room isolation is CI-proven**: contract tests assert state from room A can never appear in room B's snapshots, map documents, dice logs, or voice signaling. Lifecycle UI: rename, archive, duplicate (*New Game+*: keeps maps and NPCs, resets players), and the long-documented **Reset to Defaults** button finally ships. Multi-process sharding [BEYOND] — the Redis store is single-writer today and we don't pretend otherwise.
- **Identity: a link, never a form [LAUNCH].** The dual-password system remains the fallback, exactly as SECURITY_REQUIREMENTS demands. On top: DM-provisioned identities with single-use invite links (crypto-random ≥128-bit, HMAC-signed, revocable, **days-scale default expiry** — DMs prep Tuesday for Saturday), the **Claim Window** of identity cards with atomic first-claim-wins, and QR codes for in-person tables. Optional accounts via magic link or passkey — scoped honestly as a workstream with transactional email, recovery flows, guest-to-account claiming, and deletion/export. Guests always play; accounts are for *keeping*. After first auth, a rotating session token in an **httpOnly cookie** rides the WS upgrade, so secrets are never resent on reconnect.
- **Persistence [LAUNCH].** Whole-file JSON rewrites give way to Redis write-through plus Postgres checkpointing (60s and on idle) — named as new construction. File-backed stores remain first-class for dev and self-hosting; **pluggable storage is a stated requirement now**, because the self-host license [BEYOND] depends on it. The asset service ends the paste-a-URL era: presigned uploads to object storage, SHA-256 content addressing, MIME sniffing, caps, and quotas. Snapshots carry references, never payloads; Base64 bloat dies.
- **The Cartridge [LAUNCH].** *EJECT CARTRIDGE* downloads the whole campaign as a versioned `.htcart` — manifest, room snapshot (Atlas and SceneStates included), map documents, and **content-addressed assets bundled at export and rehosted by hash on import**, so a cart boots anywhere, including away from us. The platform auto-ejects a version every 15 active minutes, deduped by content address so thirty versions cost roughly one campaign. Restores are *drilled*, not assumed. Rendered in the UI as a gray SNES cart with a pixel label — data portability made marketable.
- **Data lifecycle [LAUNCH].** "Active" means played within 90 days. A dormant free room is auto-ejected to a cartridge, its owner emailed the download, and hot storage reclaimed. **We never delete a campaign — we hand it back.**
- **The business: the keeping tier, not the playing tier [LAUNCH].** Free forever: unlimited players, the full DM kit — vision, fog, generation, Forge included (the Foundry lesson: anything rules-critical must be first-party and free) — three active tables, 200MB. **HeroByte Plus ($4/mo, $40/yr)**: unlimited tables, 5GB, 30-version cartridge history, custom room URLs, priority warm-start. First-party asset packs $5–15 one-time. Payments are a named workstream (Stripe, tax, refunds). A published cost model — TURN bandwidth and storage are the real costs — is a launch gate. Creator marketplace with revenue share [BEYOND], sequenced last exactly as the roadmap insists. Self-host license [BEYOND].
- **Voice, honestly [LAUNCH].** TURN servers provisioned and cost-capped (the mesh's hidden recurring cost), mesh size capped around six, and voice **off by default in any public room** — P2P mesh discloses IPs to peers. SFU for large tables [BEYOND].
- **The Tavern [BEYOND, hard-gated].** The permanent public drop-in room — one click, no account, strangers and dice on a gorgeous first-party map — is the funnel and the living demo, and it ships only with its moderation kit: a constrained Tavern-DM permission subset, drawing moderation, image URL proxying, report/ban tooling, nightly reset. The concept is right; shipping it early would be shipping a griefing honeypot with our brand on it.
- **Catch-Up Replay [BEYOND, the north star for sync].** A 90-second per-room delta ring buffer lets a reconnecting player watch a fast-forward replay — tokens gliding at 8x with the SNES whir — instead of a state pop. It requires the full snapshot-to-delta migration (today only token updates ride the delta channel), and that's its value: it gives invisible infrastructure a user-visible payoff. Sequenced after the migration, never before.
- **Operations [LAUNCH].** Rolling deploys with connection draining, protocol version negotiation in the hello, reconnect backoff with jitter, a public status page, alerting with a named on-call, and quarterly restore rehearsals. The measured heartbeat RTT surfaces as a **connection HP bar** — full hearts under 80ms — telemetry with a face.

## Signature Moves

Seven things no competitor has:

1. **The Kicked-In Door.** One keystroke mid-session generates a fully compiled, playable scene — walls, doors, lights, fog — in seconds, stocked with encounter markers. Generation everywhere else is prep-time-only, in a separate app, exported by hand.
2. **The Terrain Brush.** Paint "water," get shorelines: LDtk-grade rule-based autotiling inside a live multiplayer canvas, rules pre-authored per tileset so DMs never configure anything. A category first.
3. **Living Stamps.** Prefabs that carry working doors, vision-blocking walls, and flickering lights *through publish to the play surface*. No prefab system anywhere survives that trip, because nowhere else is the editor also the table.
4. **Cartridge Codes.** Any generated world reduces to a shareable code — same code, same world, bit-identical, guaranteed by a test-enforced determinism contract. Your edits stay home.
5. **The Boss Wipe and the Victory Screen.** Combat gets a synchronized opening and closing ceremony on every screen at the table. No VTT marks these moments at all.
6. **Backstage Pockets.** An encounter is a chip you drag onstage; an armed trigger politely asks *"SPRING IT?"* Prep evaporates into a drag — first-party, no modules, travels with the save file.
7. **The Cartridge.** The entire campaign — world graph, maps, scenes, assets — ejects as one versioned file shaped like a SNES cart and boots anywhere, including away from our servers. Subscription VTTs structurally cannot copy this.

## Road to Launch

Phases, not dates. Each ends at an exit criterion, not a demo. The art track (tileset packs, palette pipeline) starts at M1 and runs in parallel — it is the longest pole.

**M0 — Playtest Steady State (now).** Finish NEXT_STEPS polish; run weekly playtests on the demo workflow. *Exit: full suite green, stable CI smokes, sessions run without operator intervention.*

**M1 — The Scene Keystone.** SceneCompiler and `CompiledScene`; live doors; server VisionService with per-recipient snapshots and vision-filtered preview channels; non-destructive republish; Map Studio JSON import. *Exit: a Map Studio dungeon publishes into a playable scene — doors click, fog works — with zero manual reconstruction; secrecy contract tests prove no leak on any channel, previews included.*

**M2 — Many Tables.** Room creation, per-room secrets, roomId routing, Redis as production default, idle unload, lifecycle UI, Reset to Defaults. *Exit: two groups play simultaneously in provable isolation; state survives restarts; the cross-room CI suite is green.*

**M3 — The Forge.** Terrain model with RLE chunks, autotiler, shared tile renderer, Tileset v1 (two packs), The Shelf, stamps, scatter brush, off-grid Alt-drag, layers/inspector chrome, hex snap, asset upload service. *Exit: the roadmap's own test — a visually complete tavern from only bundled assets; a 100x100 painted map is responsive on mid hardware; the player bundle stays inside the 175KB CI guard.*

**M4 — The Living World.** Generation domain with dungeon, building, and wilderness recipes; Atlas graph, links, SceneStates; town and world recipes; the Kicked-In Door; Cartridge Codes; basic stocking. *Exit: new world to playable encounter in under 10 minutes median (the roadmap KPI); mid-session generation under 10 seconds p95 on production hardware; identical codes produce identical worlds in CI golden tests.*

**M5 — The Table Feels Alive.** DM Deck, Pockets, Battle Strip, Boss Wipe and Victory Screen, Reveal Ritual, whispers/private rolls/handouts, Bestiary damage loop, juice-event channel and JuiceDirector, Mood music, Status Screen and macros, Save Screen recap. *Exit: an outside DM runs a full session with every repeated action at ≤2 inputs (design-review audit); juice is fully governable and reduced-motion complete.*

**M6 — Identity, Cartridge, and the Gate.** Accounts, invite links and Claim Window, co-DM schema migration, `.htcart` export/import with auto-versioning and a rehearsed restore, billing, hosting graduation off free tier, the Hardening Checklist executed, load test at 50 rooms x 6 players, onboarding docs and the long-owed demo GIF. *Exit: PRODUCTION_CHECKLIST green end to end. This is 1.0.*

**Beyond 1.0.** The Tavern (moderation-gated), Catch-Up Replay (after the delta migration), the creator marketplace, self-host license, Attract Mode, travel mode, region reroll with constraint solving, co-building, SFU voice.

## Hardening Checklist

Grounded in SECURITY_REQUIREMENTS.md and ROOM_AUTH_FLOW.md; every item is a launch gate.

**Identity and auth.** Scrypt hashing with timing-safe comparison stays; signed session tokens layer onto the handshake *without re-architecting it* (the documented design constraint), delivered as rotating httpOnly cookies; invite tokens ≥128-bit, HMAC-signed, single-use, revocable, days-scale expiry; the bootstrap-first-DM flow preserved; per-room secrets stored with room metadata; E2E coverage of the full dual-password and token flows (the documented gap, closed); per-IP rate limiting alongside the per-client token bucket; structured audit logs (pino) for every auth event, DM elevation, and destructive action, shipped through the existing metrics pipeline; HTTPS/WSS-only production with origin allowlists enforced; the demo-mode plaintext gate remains the only place a fallback secret is ever shown.

**Secrecy and isolation.** Cross-room isolation contract tests in CI across snapshots, map documents, dice, and signaling; per-recipient filter tests proving hidden NPCs, secret doors, GM notes, fog regions, and undiscovered Atlas nodes never serialize to a non-DM client — including the high-frequency preview channels.

**Persistence and data.** Redis write-through with Postgres checkpoints; atomic tmp-and-rename writes everywhere; `schemaVersion` and forward migrations on every load surface — room snapshots, cartridges, map documents, and player save files (closing the versionless PlayerState gap); combat state persisted (fixing the documented inconsistency); content-addressed cartridge versioning; quarterly restore drills; GDPR export and deletion.

**Scale and reliability.** Load test at 50 rooms x 6 players with p95 broadcast under 50ms at 8 players per room; per-room memory measured before any capacity claim is written down; idle-room unload; rolling deploys with connection draining and protocol version negotiation; reconnect storms handled with jittered backoff; status page, alerting, and a named on-call.

**Client budgets.** The 175KB gzip entry guard holds (current entry: 53KB — headroom is budgeted, not assumed); role-based code splitting preserved, with the DM Deck and Forge in lazy chunks; Lighthouse LCP/TBT/CLS budgets enforced; the photosensitivity ceiling lives in the JuiceDirector, not in per-effect discipline.

**Content and community safety.** Upload MIME sniffing, size caps, quotas, and image scanning; URL proxying for external images; report and ban tooling; a DMCA agent and process; DOMPurify sanitization on every user-string surface; voice off by default in public rooms; TURN provisioned with a cost ceiling.

**Engineering invariants.** The 350-LOC guardrail on every new module; tests-first, always; golden-snapshot determinism suites for recipes, the autotiler, and the SceneCompiler; CI smoke discipline stays few-fast-stable — the two-input rule is a design-review checklist, not a Playwright suite.
